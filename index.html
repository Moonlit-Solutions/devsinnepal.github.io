Introduction


This dashboard facilitates a comparative study of the coding landscape in Nepal with the rest of the world. This tool will help visualize and understand developers, their demographic and geographic profile, motivation, education, coding preference and several other factors.

The survey data has been utilized from StackOverflow. For almost a decade, StackOverflow’s annual Developer Survey has been the largest survey of people who code around the world. The survey is taken by nearly 65,000 people in 2020. 242 people participated from Nepal.


While Stackoverflow also publishes its analysis on the survey annually, it primarily has a wide focus on aggregated global trends. With this dashboard, Moonlit Solutions puts the spotlight on Nepali software developers and trends in Nepal.

"Fairly" is an understatement.


In fact, the median developer remuneration is the lowest in Nepal ($6300).

The median remuneration in the US is $115,000. Broken into hourly pay (40 hours in a week, 52 weeks in a year), a Nepali developer makes $3.03 an hour where as a developer in the US makes $55.30 an hour. It is not suprising to see so many gifted and talented pool of devs move for better opportunities.


This also implies that Nepal is an untapped market for outsourcing. ...........



Messy data is inevitible


Survey data is prone to errors, especially when multiple inputs need to be collected to create a calculated field. Developers have hourly, weekly, monthly or yearly wage/salaries. Additionally, since StackOverflow collects responses from all over the world, the currency in which developers are compensated needs to be recorded as well. Eventually, for analysis, the data will be standardized (all remuneration will be yearly, and converted in U.S dollars). However, the datasets reflect various inconsistencies in this section of the survey, given that the inputs are used to create a standard calculated field.

The realization started with us trying to confirm a high positive correlation between years of experience and developer compensation. Based on heuristics alone, this is anticipated. As someone becomes more skillful, their compensation is expected to grow as well.


Since different countries pay differently, we initially wanted to study the difference in correlation coefficients (experience vs pay) between Nepal and the rest of the world. But, we stumbled upon something else.



It is very strange to see ceiling patterns in 1000K (a million dollars) and 2000K (2 million dollars). There are two (non mutually excusive) possible reasons. It is entirely possible for responders to round up their compensation. But, since this is a calculated field, a more viable reason seems that this was forced during calculation.
